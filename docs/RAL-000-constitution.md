# RAL-000: Research AI Lab Constitution

## Purpose

Research AI Lab exists to build open, rigorous, and durable methodological infrastructure for AI-assisted scientific research.

The initiative is not defined by a single tool, model, or platform. It is defined by a public commitment to better research practice under conditions of increasing information abundance and increasing AI capability.

## Principles

- Open science orientation
- Methodological rigor
- Evidence-driven reasoning
- Vendor neutrality
- Reproducibility by design
- Community-first development
- Long-term institutional thinking

## Governance

Research AI Lab should operate with lightweight but explicit governance.

Governance functions:

- Protect the mission
- Maintain documentation standards
- Review structural changes
- Curate official labs and toolkit components
- Encourage open contribution while preserving coherence

Recommended governance layers:

- Maintainers: steward architecture and quality
- Contributors: improve documents, labs, examples, and resources
- Reviewers: provide domain or methodological feedback
- Community participants: discuss, test, adapt, and extend the work

## Decision Making

Decision making should optimize for transparency and continuity.

Default model:

- Editorial changes: maintainer review
- Methodological changes: documented rationale plus review
- Structural changes: explicit proposal and consensus-seeking discussion
- Mission-level changes: high threshold, publicly justified

When consensus is not possible, maintainers are responsible for deciding in ways that preserve the constitution and long-term coherence of the initiative.

## Community Values

- Curiosity with discipline
- Openness with standards
- Ambition with humility
- Collaboration with accountability
- Accessibility with seriousness

## Open Science Philosophy

Research AI Lab supports open scientific culture wherever legally, ethically, and practically possible.

This means valuing:

- Transparent methods
- Source traceability
- Shared learning artifacts
- Reusable research workflows
- Public methodological discussion

Open science does not mean lowering quality thresholds. It means making quality legible, repeatable, and shareable.

## Responsible AI-Assisted Research

Responsible AI-assisted research within Research AI Lab means:

- AI may accelerate research tasks, but not replace scholarly responsibility
- Claims must remain attributable to evidence, not merely plausible output
- Human judgment remains accountable for framing, interpretation, and conclusions
- Researchers should disclose AI assistance when appropriate to context and policy
- Workflows should minimize hallucination, citation drift, hidden bias, and false confidence

## Quality Standards

Official Research AI Lab materials should be:

- Clear
- Structured
- Reproducible
- Referenced when substantive claims are made
- Explicit about limitations
- Useful beyond a single moment or tool cycle

## Contribution Principles

Contributions should improve the initiative as shared infrastructure.

Contributors are expected to:

- Respect the constitution
- Improve clarity or rigor
- Avoid unnecessary fragmentation
- Document assumptions
- Keep the initiative future-proof
