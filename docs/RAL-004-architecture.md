# RAL-004: Architecture

## Overview

Research AI Lab is designed as a layered initiative in which each component reinforces the next.

```text
Research AI Lab
  -> Keynotes
  -> Toolkit
  -> Labs
  -> Blueprints
  -> Guides
  -> Prompts
  -> Resources
  -> Community
  -> Academy
  -> Future Research Agents
```

## Component Model

### Research AI Lab

The institutional umbrella. It defines the mission, standards, identity, governance, and long-term coherence of the initiative.

### Keynotes

Public narratives that translate the initiative for broad audiences. Keynotes communicate why the project matters, but they are downstream from the architecture, not a substitute for it.

### Toolkit

The practical methodological layer. The toolkit provides canvases, checklists, matrices, workflow sheets, prompt libraries, and reference assets that researchers can actually use.

### Labs

Applied research domains that implement the methodology on real questions. Labs are where the architecture becomes concrete and testable.

### Blueprints

Structured templates for recurring workflows, such as literature review setup, evidence synthesis, policy analysis, or comparative evaluation.

### Guides

Instructional documents that teach how to use the architecture, toolkit, and labs effectively.

### Prompts

Curated prompt patterns aligned with documented research stages. Prompts are treated as operational aids, not substitutes for methodology.

### Resources

Supporting materials such as glossaries, reading lists, links, reference datasets, and external tools.

### Community

The social layer of the initiative. Community turns documents into an evolving public practice through contribution, peer learning, feedback, and collaborative refinement.

### Academy

A future educational layer that can support teaching, training, certification, and structured learning pathways.

### Future Research Agents

An advanced future layer in which bounded agents help execute well-documented parts of the research workflow under explicit guardrails, traceability, and human review.

## Architectural Principles

- Layers should reinforce one another
- Educational assets should emerge from real methodology
- Labs should validate toolkit usefulness
- Future agent systems must remain accountable to documented standards
- The architecture must support expansion without losing identity
