# RAL-008: Research Lab Specification

## Definition

A Research Lab is a domain-specific implementation of the Research AI Lab methodology around a concrete, high-value research question.

Each lab should serve both as:

- A practical research workflow
- A teaching artifact for how rigorous AI-assisted research can be done

## Required Components

Every official lab must contain the following sections.

### Research Question

A clearly framed question with intellectual and practical relevance.

### Objectives

Specific goals for the inquiry and expected learning outcomes.

### Background

Context that explains why the question matters and what debates or uncertainties surround it.

### Keywords

Core terms, synonyms, and related concepts to support search design.

### Search Strategy

Databases, search operators, inclusion logic, and evidence collection approach.

### Research Workflow

A staged method from framing through synthesis and reflection.

### Toolchain

Suggested tools, with vendor-neutral framing when possible.

### Expected Outputs

What the lab should produce, such as evidence matrices, annotated bibliographies, synthesis memos, visual summaries, or research briefs.

### Critical Analysis

Questions that force evaluation of evidence quality, assumptions, and contradictions.

### Research Gaps

Important unanswered questions or underexplored areas identified during the workflow.

### Prompts

Prompt patterns aligned with specific stages of the methodology.

### References

Initial or exemplary sources, where appropriate.

### Checklist

A concise completion and quality-control checklist.

### Reflection Questions

Prompts for methodological reflection and learning.

### Reproducibility Notes

Requirements for documenting steps, decisions, tools, and limitations so the workflow can be repeated and audited.
